Why Messy Play Matters

Nurturing Brains Through Squish & Squash

Messy play is more than just fun. It is a vital pathway for child growth. Squeezing, pouring, and exploring different textures sparks neurological connections, physical agility, and emotional comfort.

💪

Fine & Gross Motor Skills

Squeezing soft clay, scooping slippery rice, and pouring water build finger strength, grip coordination, and overall physical agility.

🧠

Cognitive & Language Growth

Children naturally categorize shapes, experiment with cause-and-effect, and discover descriptive new words like "slimey", "sticky", and "crunchy".

🤝

Social & Emotional Well-being

Our sessions build sensory resilience, reduce tactile sensitivity, and offer a cooperative, stress-free zone for children and parents to connect.

Common Parent Questions

Squish Squash Studio FAQs

Got questions about messy play? We've got answers. If you don't find what you need here, feel free to pop us a WhatsApp message!

We recommend wearing old clothes that you don't mind getting paint or food coloring on. While we provide high-coverage, toddler-sized protective aprons, sensory play is a full-body sport! Bringing a spare change of clothes and a small towel for the wash station is highly recommended.

Yes, absolutely! The safety of your little ones is our top priority. All sensory bins, rainbow spaghetti, jellies, and play doughs are made with 100% non-toxic, edible-safe, and food-grade kitchen ingredients. While we don't encourage eating them as a snack, they are fully taste-safe!

Our studio has curated setups for child milestones:
• Sensory Babies: 6 to 18 months (focus on soft lights, water exploration, edible slimes, texture exploration).
• Squish Squash Toddlers: 18 months to 4 years (more advanced pouring, stacking, matching, painting, shaving foam).
• Family Messy Play: 6 months to 6 years (great for siblings to play and explore together).
